Avionics Maintenance Technician
BAE Systems 12·2013 - Present

I am an Avionic Maintenance technician with three years on aircraft experience performing routine servicing, fault investigation, defect rectification and modification embodiment in accordance with EASA Mil Part 45 regulations. These tasks encompass termination of fibre optic, coaxial and electrical cables, strip, rebuild and functional testing of avionic and electrical systems including: Navigation, Flight Control, Communications, Radar, Electrical Power and Armament Systems.

RAF Coningsby 08·2014-Present
Eurofighter Typhoon

For this role I work within an EASA Mil Part 145 environment alongside BAE, RAF and subcontractor personnel as directed by the trade Supervisor and in line with all relevant COSHH and risk assessments. In August 2015 I successfully gained Typhoon Q accreditation and regularly apply this system knowledge to day to day working and fault investigation tasks. A large part of my role is to assist with on the job training of current apprentices, instilling sound engineering practices, good husbandry and an understanding of working within regulations to ensure safety and accountability are upheld at all times.

RAF Vaalley 02·2014-08·2014
Hawk T2

A thouroughly enjoyable six month secondment to RAF Valley as part of the avionics team, performing depth maintenance on HAWK T2 Aircraft in support of the existing work force as directed by the Trade Supervisor. Worked within EASA Mil Part 45 regulations alongside Babcock’s personnel, ensuring that all relevant policies were adhered to. Liaised with the Part M to ensure that bespoke wiring repairs were carried out accurately and were sufficient to rectify the fault.

RAF Marham 01·2013-02·2014
Tornado GR4

Continued as part of the avionics team on Tornado GR4 Aircraft following the completion of my NVQ. This role involved performing fault investigation, defect rectification and routine servicing as directed by the Shift Supervisor using the BAE Direct Manufacturing System (DMS) for electronic work documentation. Ensured that all work was carried out in accordance with EASA Mil Part 45 regulations and that all relevant safety precautions regulations were adhered to.
